We introduce ViTCM-LLM: To diagnose like a real TCM physician

Abstract

Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) diagnosis, particularly methods like tongue diagnosis, faces significant challenges in subjectivity and scalability. The application of Large Language Models (LLMs) to fundamental aspect of TCM tasks such as syndrome differentiation (Bian Zheng) and prescription generation is significantly challenged by the difficulty of integrating visual tongue data with clinical text and the scarcity of suitable public datasets.

To overcome these barriers, we introduce ViTCM-LLM, a novel framework that emulates an expert's diagnostic process by integrating multimodal language modeling with Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). Employing a dual-component architecture, ViTCM-LLM integrates a fine-tuned Qwen2.5-VL model for visual analysis with a Qwen3-based RAG for clinical reasoning.

The entire framework was developed and validated on MedTCM, a new large-scale multimodal dataset we introduce specifically for advanced TCM research. To properly evaluate our framework's clinical accuracy, which existing metrics fail to capture, we also developed TDEU, a domain-specific evaluation metric. Evaluated using this metric alongside standard benchmarks, our comprehensive experiments on MedTCM demonstrate that ViTCM-LLM significantly outperforms leading models, including GPT-4o and Gemini 2.5 Flash. These findings not only establish the feasibility of a generalizable tongue diagnosis model but also validate the critical role of integrating visual data for advanced TCM diagnostics.

Related Work

Framework Overview

Our work is divided into three stages. First is the Tongue Diagnosis Generator. This component uses a fine-tuned multimodal model (Qwen2.5-VL) to analyze a patient's tongue image and generate a single-sentence tongue diagnosis. It is specifically prompted to act as a Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) tongue diagnosis expert to ensure a structured and professional output.

The second stage is the Prescription Generator. This component uses an advanced Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) framework based on the Qwen3 model. It combines the tongue diagnosis result from the first stage with the patient's other clinical information (chief complaint, present history, pulse diagnosis). This combined data is used to retrieve similar cases from a large clinical knowledge base of over 124,000 anonymized patient records. Based on the patient's data and the retrieved cases, the model generates a final output that includes a diagnosis, dialectical analysis, a specific herbal prescription with dosages, and the reasoning for the diagnosis.

Finally, we introduce a new, domain-specific evaluation metric called TDEU (Tongue Diagnosis Evaluation Unit). This metric is designed to overcome the limitations of standard text-comparison metrics like BLEU or ROUGE, which cannot capture the clinical nuances of TCM terminology.

Results

Our results achieve SOTA performance in both objective evaluation metrics and Traditional Chinese Medicine expert evaluation.
